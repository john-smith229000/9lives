# Day 4 — The Rats / The Goal Locks (beat outline)

Day 4: the objective crystallizes, and you spend the day catching rats — satisfying, but
they'll be back, because you're treating the symptom. **Beats are direction + intent, not
scripted lines.** `[GAME]` = mechanic foregrounded. Cross-refs `arc.md` (Day 4),
`chain_quests.md` (Day 4 puzzle), `../design/objects_and_kinetics.md`. Roles only.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** deal with the rats plaguing the fish seller, the rocks nest
  (low tide), the retiree's shed, and the cottage — bait the trap, catch each, carry it out.
- **Shown, not told:** the two-caretaker mirror deepening (you tend the keeper's sick partner
  the way you tend your friend); the mother waiting on someone the sea keeps away; that
  catching rats one by one never lasts.
- **If stuck (hints):** the fish seller states the rat problem and how the trap works; the
  low-tide window is signposted and the water is visibly pulled back; highlight the trap and
  the rat's flee-lanes.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **fish seller** (catch being stolen; the schedule); the **retiree**
(shed rat; a shortcut/lore reward); the **mother** (a pantry rat; waiting on the sea); the
**keeper** and their **sick partner** (a rat by the bed; the keeper thaws a degree).

## MORNING
1. **Home — wake.** Same dawn. Player takes fish for the friend; convey the leak deeper
   still — they pick at it, leave more than half. The cat also has the **trap** now
   (`has_rat_trap` persisted). Leave with purpose.
2. **Fish market — the problem stated.** The fish seller has only scraps again — the rats.
   Convey: no good fish for the friend until the rats are handled. The day's task is set.

*→ phase tick: MORNING → MIDDAY.*

## MIDDAY  *(the tide is out — the rocks are reachable)*
3. **Catch #1 — the market.** `[GAME] lure & trap + carry constraint.` Carry a **fish** to
   the **trap** and bait it (you hold the fish *or* the caught rat, never both — sequence
   the trips). Position so the rat's flee-lane runs into the trap; spring it; **carry the
   rat out** and release it at the sea wall (a small kindness — the cat relocates, doesn't
   kill). Convey the fish seller's gruff gratitude; she lets slip more of the **ship
   schedule / when it's due** (reinforcing the vet from Day 3).
4. **Catch #2 — the rocks nest.** `[GAME] low-tide gate (signposted).` The fish seller's tip
   points to a nest on the rocks reachable only at **low tide (midday)** — the water is
   visibly pulled back. Cross, bait, catch, release. Convey the town's counting of tides
   (the sea's absence measured in exposed rock).

## AFTERNOON
5. **Optional catches — the retiree's shed & the mother's pantry.** The retiree's shed rat
   pays in a **shortcut** (a persistent path that shaves a later loop) + a **scrap of lore**
   about the light "before." The mother's pantry rat is a warm, ordinary-life beat — convey
   her keeping a lamp lit for someone the shut sea won't give back (decoupled from the
   keeper). Both optional colour.
6. **The keeper's foot cottage — the sick partner.** A rat has been troubling the keeper's
   **sick partner**; you're kept at the doorway, but you catch it by the bed. Convey the
   mirror made literal — you tend their loved one the way you tend your friend — and the
   **keeper watching**, softening a degree (first crack; seeds `keeper_trust`, begun Day 5).
7. **The goal locks (thought/summary beat).** The whole shape is now plain: the ship (with
   the vet) can't dock because the beacon is dark; the beacon is dark because the keeper is
   **the only one** who can care for their partner and cannot leave to restore and tend it.
   Convey the weight of clarity — you finally see the whole machine you have to move.

*→ phase tick: AFTERNOON → EVENING.*

## EVENING
8. **The symptom, not the cure (frustration beat).** Convey the sinking realization that the
   rats will simply be back tomorrow — you've eased four yards for a day, not fixed anything.
   This frustration is the hook into Day 5's permanent seal. Head home.

## NIGHT
9. **Home — sleep.** With the market's good fish finally available, you bring the friend a
   proper piece — and convey the cruelty of timing: they leave most of it. Curl up → fade →
   loop.
10. **Wake — loop to Day 5.** Same dawn. The cat knows *where* the rats come from now
    (yesterday's nests), and that catching them one by one is a losing game — carrying the
    day's frustration into a plan.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** the retiree's **shortcut** (path), and **knowledge** of the nest / hole
  locations. The individual **catches reset each loop** — the rats return (intended:
  symptom, not source). `keeper_trust` cracked open (not yet earned).
- **Knowledge / goal:** the full objective is locked (beacon + berth → ship → vet; keeper
  gates the beacon). The nest knowledge points at the sealable **source behind the wharf**
  (Day 5).
- **Leak:** friend leaves most of the fish — the clearest notch yet.
