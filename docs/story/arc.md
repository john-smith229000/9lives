# Days 2–9 — Development & Weave

Continues `../design/quest_web.md`. Nameless, role-based. This pass threads the mechanical web
through the emotional arcs and tightens the loop's logic.

## The heart of it: an imperfect loop, and the friend is the leak

The day resets — the town, the tides, the townsfolk, the broken things — **almost**
perfectly. The one thing the loop can't hold is the thing you most want it to. **Each loop
the friend is a shade more lethargic than the last.** They eat the fish you bring, then pick
at it, then leave it, then wave it off — *"not hungry… you have it."* The ear that used to
stay up when they pretended to sleep starts staying down. The reset restores the world but
keeps letting the friend slip.

This is the real engine:

- **The loop was never safe.** Freezing time was your desperate attempt to protect them —
  and it's failing. Love and fear can delay the world, but they can't actually stop this.
  The slow decline is the truth seeping through the cracks.
- So there's a **soft, dreadful clock**: you can't loop forever. The longer the chain takes
  to mend, the weaker the friend is when the ship finally comes. (Emotional pressure, not a
  fail state — never a game-over; the decline is dialogue and behaviour, per the
  no-mechanical-punishment rule.)
- The **terrible bargain sharpens.** The only way to truly help is to **let the loop break**
  and let tomorrow come — but tomorrow is when the friend is most at risk, and now they're
  weaker than when you began. Clinging to the loop dooms them slowly; breaking it risks them
  all at once. Hoping *is* risking — and now it's urgent.
- The **keeper mirrors this exactly**: they stopped tending the light because hoping meant
  risking loss. You become able to do the thing they couldn't — and, by Day 8, drag them to
  it beside you.
- **Why it leaks stays unexplained** (love and fear, never fully told). The loop holds
  everything except the one it was built to hold.

### The leak — how to show it (a progression, not a meter)

Tie the tells loosely to how many loops have passed, so the friend visibly slips. Keep it in
behaviour and dialogue — never a health bar:

- **Early (Days 1–3):** eats the fish; teases you; sends you out; the ear stays up when they
  fake sleep so you'll go.
- **Middle (Days 4–6):** picks at the fish, eats half; shorter replies; asleep when you get
  home; "…just resting my eyes."
- **Late (Days 7–8):** leaves the fish untouched — *"you have it"*; barely lifts their head;
  the ear stays down; stops asking for "something to look at."
- The cruelty is in the **timing**: you fix the town's fish supply right as they stop eating;
  you learn to bring the perfect small gift right as they stop looking.

## The parallel arcs (what's actually progressing while the web unlocks)

| Thread | Start | Middle | End |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mechanical spine** | notice the broken things | unlock the web, fixes persist | beacon lit + berth clear → ship |
| **You (orange cat)** | tenderness, search | the horror of the loop → grim, tireless persistence → a low point | learning to hope = letting tomorrow come |
| **The friend** | deflecting, eating the fish, ear up | fading loop to loop — less appetite, shorter words, more sleep; half-noticing you're strange | barely eating, ear down; a last frightened honesty |
| **The keeper** | despair, given up | grudging trust as you ease their burden | chooses hope the same hour you do |
| **The mother** *(separate)* | waiting on someone the sea keeps away | small warmth as the town mends | relief when the ship comes |
| **The town** | held breath, empty chairs | visibly healing as fixes accumulate | alive, lamps gold, the quay full again |
| **Loop-awareness** | only you remember | the café/bookshop keeper starts to *sense* it | never fully explained — left as love and fear |

The trick is to run all seven in parallel so a "puzzle day" is always also a character day.

## Townsfolk relationship hooks (Day-1 personalities → payoffs)
Each townsperson's Day-1 attitude (see `cast.md`) turns over the loop:
- **Grocer** — hostile and cat-hating on Day 1 (sure you're a thief). When you **catch the
  rat raiding his store (Day 4–5)** he flips to grudging, then genuine, warmth — the one cat
  he trusts. His cold open makes the turn land; and it's *why* the oil/trap access loosens.
- **Café owner** — a sweet cat-lover from the start; a **recurring source of comfort gifts**
  (treats and little things for the friend), so she quietly feeds G3 (the friend's care and
  the "something to look at" ritual) across the whole run.
- **Marina worker** — lazy on Day 1; your persistence **shames then inspires** him, so his
  slip/bridge help feels earned by the mid-game.
- **Dock master** — wound tight with stress; his knots **loosen as the harbour mends**
  (cleared berth, a fillable register line) — a visible barometer of the town's recovery.
- **Retirees** — the "kept hoping" bickering couple; memory + comic relief throughout.

## Loop-awareness — a quiet escalating thread

Keep it understated; never explain the why.

- **You** carry everything from Day 1.
- The **café/bookshop keeper** is the one who feels it: early, "the evenings just close
  over"; mid-arc, "…haven't we sat here before?"; late, they stop being surprised when you
  know things, and simply help. They come closest to the truth and never quite name it.
- **Others** get uncanny flickers — the dock master's pen hovering over the same unfilled
  line; a retiree telling "the same story, was it only yesterday?"; the friend, near the
  end: "You keep looking at me like you're saying goodbye. …Have we done this?"
- Payoff: you're never *believed*, exactly — you're **trusted**, which is warmer and sadder.

## Days 2–9

Each mid-day: one mechanic foregrounded, one **persistent** change, one character deepened.

### Day 2 — Searching *(mechanic: push / help)*
- **Emotional core:** you're sure now you're looping; you scour the town for a cure and hit
  dead ends. The panic under the calm.
- **Unlocks:** help the **marina hand** free a jammed slip → **crates freed** *(persists)*.
- **Beats:** meet the **keeper** properly at the tower's foot — you can't get up, but you
  see the **sick loved one** through the lit window, and the keeper turning you away, gentle
  and hollow. Bring the friend fish (scraps still — the rats).
- **Threads:** keeper introduced as the mirror; you don't understand yet that helping them
  and lighting the beacon are the same act.
- **Persists:** `slip_cleared`, `crates_freed`.

### Day 3 — Reaching *(mechanic: stack + jump)*
- **Emotional core:** the first real forward motion — and the first flicker of hope you
  distrust.
- **Unlocks:** box-bridge + jump to the **tree** (the beacon's fallen **brass lens
  fitting**) and to the **grocer's** storm-stranded stock → **oil + a rat trap** *(persist)*.
- **Beats:** the **fish seller** / **café keeper** let the truth surface: the ship that's
  "due" carries a **traveling vet**. The thing you've been circling has a name.
- **Threads:** the café keeper's first uncanny line ("…haven't we?"). You realize the ship
  is the hope — and that the ship can't come while the light is dark.
- **Persists:** `lens_recovered`, `oil_secured`, `has_rat_trap`, `learned_vet`.

### Day 4 — The goal locks *(mechanic: catch / timing)*
- **Emotional core:** clarity, and the weight of it. You finally see the whole shape:
  beacon dark + berth fouled = no ship; beacon dark because the keeper can't leave the bed.
- **Unlocks:** start **catching rats** (trap) for the fish seller, the keeper's foot cottage,
  a retiree / the mother's pantry — satisfying, but they **come back** (symptom, not cause).
- **Beats:** the **foot cottage** — you catch the rat by the keeper's **sick partner's** bed
  while the keeper *notices*; the mirror made literal (you tend their loved one as you tend
  yours). The **mother** is a lighter, separate echo (waiting on someone the sea keeps away).
- **Threads:** the two-caretaker mirror (you ↔ the keeper) tightening.
- **Persists:** knowledge (where the rats bite hardest) — no permanent fix yet.

### Day 5 — The clever fix *(mechanic: the persistent seal)*
- **Emotional core:** the joy of a fix that *stays* — the first time the town gets easier.
- **Unlocks:** a retiree / the café keeper lets slip the **rat-hole behind the wharf**;
  reach it via the box-bridge; **seal the source** *(persists)*. One fix, several yards eased
  at once — the fish seller's catch safe, the mother's pantry and the retiree's shed quiet,
  the keeper's **sick partner** calmed.
- **Beats:** with real fish at last, you start bringing it to the keeper's **sick partner**
  too, and catching the rat by their bed — the keeper watching, saying little. The cruel
  timing lands here: you finally have the *good* fish, and the friend has begun to leave it.
  You bring it anyway.
- **Threads:** "small kindnesses hold the place together," now literal; the keeper's thaw
  begins.
- **Persists:** `rat_source_sealed`, `keeper_trust` (begun).

### Day 6 — The berth *(mechanic: crates + leverage / combined)*
- **Emotional core:** turning outward — fixing the town's face to the sea.
- **Unlocks:** help the **dock master** clear the fouled berth (a sunk skiff / jammed gate,
  moved with crates + leverage) → **berth usable** *(persists)*.
- **Beats:** the dock master, all procedure, quietly updates the register — the first time
  the unfilled line looks fillable. A crack in his bureaucratic armor.
- **Threads:** the town starting to believe; lamps a touch brighter; a chair or two filled
  at the cook's.
- **Persists:** `berth_cleared`.

### Day 7 — The beacon *(mechanic: everything combined)*
- **Emotional core:** so close it frightens you.
- **Unlocks:** the **marina hand** makes the cliff bridge **permanent**; you reinstall the
  **lens** (from the tree) and haul the **oil** up; the keeper's trust becomes the **tower
  key**. Everything is ready but the flame.
- **Beats:** the keeper lets you into the lantern room — and voices the despair you've been
  carrying: *"You can light it. It won't bring anything in. Nothing comes when you call."*
  The mirror speaks your own fear back to you.
- **Threads:** your **low point** lives here or the next morning — the exhaustion of doing
  everything right and still standing in the dark, and the dawning knowledge that the last
  step isn't a repair. It's **letting go**.
- **Persists:** `bridge_permanent`, `beacon_ready`, `has_tower_key`.

### Day 8 — The turn *(the choice, not a mechanic)*
- **Emotional core:** to light it, you both have to choose to hope — to let tomorrow come,
  knowing time resumes and the friend is truly at risk again.
- **Beats:** you strike the light at dusk. The keeper, beside you, chooses it too — for
  their loved one, for the town, and because you wouldn't let them not. The beam sweeps out
  into the fog for the first time in a long time.
- **Threads:** the friend, that night: *"You keep looking at me like you're saying goodbye.
  …Have we done this?"* You let the day end, on purpose, for the first time.
- **Persists:** `beacon_lit`. (And, quietly, the loop's grip loosening.)

### Day 9 — The ship *(resolution)*
- **Emotional core:** release — but earned through a real near-thing, because the friend is
  at their weakest this morning (the leak's final cost of every loop you spent).
- **Beats:** dawn comes **different** — and the friend barely stirs. For a breath you wonder
  if you waited too long. Then the fog parts; a horn — the sound the whole game has been
  missing. The ship finds the light, takes the cleared berth, ties up. The vet comes ashore.
  The friend (and the keeper's loved one) is reached in time. The town, healed by a hundred
  small mends, is out on the quay in the gold.
- **Threads:** the arrival lands *because* the loop was failing — you didn't break it when
  it was safe, you broke it when staying had become the greater danger. That's the courage
  the whole arc was teaching.
- **Threads:** you never learn *why* the days froze — only that they've thawed. Love and
  fear, never fully explained. *(Ending tone still to lock — see below.)*

## Weaving devices (recurring objects & callbacks)

- **"Bring me something to look at."** The friend's Day-1 request becomes a tiny evolving
  ritual — each loop a different small thing (a shell, a feather, the fish seller's
  paper-wrapped scrap). Late loops they say "it's lovely" without looking; near the end they
  stop asking — the ritual fading with them. On the last night you bring the thing that
  means the most, and whether they look is the whole game in a beat.
- **The fish.** The clearest tell of the leak: eaten → picked at → left → refused ("you have
  it"). The kindest thing you can carry, quietly stopping being enough — the loop failing,
  rendered in one recurring object.
- **The cook's set-aside bowl / the café's second cup / the dock master's unfilled line.**
  Three images of *waiting*, planted Day 1, each resolved on Day 9 (the bowl eaten, the cup
  drunk, the line filled with a docked ship's name).
- **The lens fitting's journey:** fell from the tower → lodged in the town's tree → carried
  back up by you → catches the flame on Day 8. A piece of the light that took the whole town
  to put back.
- **The rat:** symptom (caught, returns) → source (sealed, gone). The shape of real help.
- **The box-bridge:** rickety → permanent. The town getting easier as it heals.
- **The horn's absence → the horn.** Day 1's defining non-sound; Day 9's first sound.

## The low point (place it Day 7 night / Day 8 dawn)

Everything mended, and still dark. The realization that no repair remains — the last step is
to *stop protecting the friend by freezing time* and let tomorrow risk them. And by now the
loop has shown its hand: the friend is barely eating, barely lifting their head. Staying is
no longer safe — the loop is slowly taking them anyway. So letting go is both the most
terrifying thing and the only mercy. The keeper gives it words. You've spent eight days
learning to do the one thing fear won't let you: hope out loud. This is the emotional
climax; Day 9 is the exhale.
