# Day 5 — The Clever Seal (beat outline)

Day 5: the first fix that *stays* — and the first time the town gets easier. **Beats are
direction + intent, not scripted lines.** `[GAME]` = mechanic foregrounded. Cross-refs
`arc.md`, `chain_quests.md` (Day 5). Roles only.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** find and seal the rats' source behind the wharf (reached at
  low tide) — plank it over and weigh it shut.
- **Shown, not told:** one fix quieting four yards (the town gets easier); the keeper
  thawing; the cruel timing (good fish at last, and the friend leaves it).
- **If stuck (hints):** a retiree / the café keeper mentions the hole behind the wharf; the
  low-tide window is signposted; highlight the plank and the plate.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **fish seller**, **retiree**, **mother** (the yards the seal
quiets); the keeper's **sick partner** (fish delivery); the **keeper** (thaw begins).

## MORNING
1. **Home — wake with a plan.** Same dawn; the cat rises already knowing where the rats come
   from (yesterday's frustration → today's plan). Fish to the friend (leak: they take a
   bite, no more). Leave.
2. **The realization stated.** A quick beat that convey the intent: stop chasing rats, close
   the hole they pour from. Head for the wharf.

*→ phase tick: MORNING → MIDDAY (the tide goes out).*

## MIDDAY  *(low tide — the wharf underside is reachable)*
3. **Reach the hole.** `[GAME] box-bridge + low-tide gate.` Cross to behind the wharf using
   the freed crates (bridge) at low tide. Convey the grim little space where it all starts.
4. **Seal it.** `[GAME] carry + plate-weighted seal.` **Carry** a driftwood **plank** to the
   hole and lay it over. It won't hold alone — push a **crate onto a plate** beside it (or
   tip a **barrel** to rest against it) to **weigh it shut**. → `rat_source_sealed`.
5. **The ripple.** Convey the single-fix payoff immediately: the fish seller's slab now holds
   a real catch (a visible healing state), the retiree's shed and the mother's pantry go
   quiet, and — crucially — the keeper's foot cottage calms. "Small kindnesses hold the place
   together," made literal.

*→ phase tick: MIDDAY → AFTERNOON.*

## AFTERNOON
6. **The foot cottage — comfort the partner.** With a proper catch available, carry fish up
   to the keeper's **sick partner**; the rat by the bed is already gone (you sealed the
   source). For the first time you're let close enough to **curl up beside them** a while —
   the cat's own kind of comfort. The **keeper** begins to thaw in earnest → `keeper_trust`
   (begun). Convey the mirror tightening: you comfort their loved one the way you do yours.
7. **The cruel timing (quiet beat).** You finally have the *good* fish for the friend — and
   this is the day they start to leave it. Convey it without comment; let the object carry it.

*→ phase tick: AFTERNOON → EVENING.*

## EVENING
8. **Walk home.** Convey the strange lightness of a fix that held vs. the weight of the
   friend fading. Two truths at once.

## NIGHT
9. **Home — sleep.** Bring the good fish; the friend leaves most of it, but is glad you sat.
   Curl up → fade → loop.
10. **Wake — loop to Day 6.** The rats are gone *and stay gone* now; the cat wakes into a
    quieter, slightly-easier town. Turns outward: the ship still can't dock.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** `rat_source_sealed` (rats gone for good — market catch reliable, cottage
  calm); `keeper_trust` begun.
- **Town heals:** fish slab full; a quiet market (see `../design/town_structures.md` §4).
- **Leak:** friend leaves most of the fish.
