# Day 8 — The Turn (beat outline)

Day 8: short and weighty — to light the beacon you both have to choose to hope, and to let
tomorrow finally come. **Beats are direction + intent, not scripted lines.** `[GAME]` =
mechanic foregrounded. Cross-refs `arc.md`, `../design/days_2_to_9` note. Roles only.
Target length: ~10–12 min (fewer puzzles, more weight).

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** fetch flint, climb the tower, and light the beacon.
- **Shown, not told:** the choice to hope, shown in action not speech; the keeper joining
  you; letting the day end on purpose.
- **If stuck (hints):** the grocer / a hearth for the flint; the lamp clearly prompts the
  strike.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **keeper** (beside you at the flame); the town (its lights
answering, later). Emotional climax — minimal mechanics.

## MORNING
1. **Home — wake into the last day (that you'll allow).** Same dawn; the friend is at their
   frailest yet. Convey that the cat has decided — today the light, whatever it costs.
   Bring the fish; convey they can't take it now.
2. **One last fetch.** `[GAME] carry.` Gather **flint & tinder** (a single carry — from the
   grocer / a hearth) for the flame. Small, deliberate; the calm before.

*→ phase tick: MORNING → toward dusk (progress-driven; convey the day drawing down).*

## AFTERNOON → DUSK  *(the climb)*
3. **Up the tower.** Walk the permanent stair to the ready lantern room (interior). Convey
   the quiet — everything's been leading here.
4. **The keeper.** The keeper is there, still not believing. Convey, without argument, that
   the cat doesn't try to convince them — just refuses to leave / starts the work. The
   choice is shown in action, not speech.
5. **Strike the flame.** `[GAME] use (the capstone).` A single, deliberate **strike** lights
   the lamp. → `beacon_lit`. The beam sweeps out into the fog for the first time in a long
   while. Convey the enormity of a small action.
6. **The keeper chooses it too.** Convey the keeper, at the rail, letting themselves hope
   beside you — for their partner, for the town, because you wouldn't let them not. The two
   mirrors align: you both stop freezing and start hoping.

## NIGHT
7. **Home — the last night of the loop.** Convey the friend's near-recognition — that this
   has happened before, that you look like you're saying goodbye. And convey the cat's
   choice: to let the day *end* on purpose for the first time — to stop protecting them by
   freezing time, because staying has become the greater danger. Curl up → fade.
8. **The loop loosens.** Convey (through the fade / the wake) that this dawn will be
   different — the grip is letting go.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** `beacon_lit`; the loop's hold loosening (sets Day 9's *different* dawn).
- **The turn:** hope chosen, by the cat and the keeper, in the same hour. The thematic
  climax; Day 9 is the exhale.
- **Leak:** at its worst — which is *why* letting go is both terrifying and the only mercy.
