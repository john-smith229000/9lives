# Day 9 — The Ship (beat outline)

Day 9: the exhale. The loop breaks; the ship comes in; help arrives — earned as a real
near-thing. **Beats are direction + intent, not scripted lines.** Cross-refs `arc.md`
(Day 9), `../design/town_structures.md` §4 (the healed town). Roles only. Short (~8–10 min),
mostly traversal + payoff, little to no puzzle.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** head down to the dock as the ship arrives, then bring the vet
  to the friend.
- **Shown, not told:** the healed town (the opening image inverted); the near-too-late; that
  the *why* is never explained.
- **If stuck (hints):** the horn and the gathering crowd draw you to the dock; follow the vet
  home.

## Cast in play
The **friend**; the **vet** (off the ship — the only outsider); the **keeper** and their
**sick partner**; the whole town (out, at last).

## MORNING — different
1. **Wake — the dawn changes.** For the first time the framing breaks: the light is
   different, the fog thinner. Convey the friend barely stirring — the leak's final cost of
   every loop spent. Convey a held-breath fear: did you wait too long?
2. **The horn.** Convey the sound the whole game has withheld — a ship's horn out in the
   parting fog. The beam still turning overhead found the mouth in the night.

## MIDDAY — the ship
3. **Down to the dock.** Walk the healed town to the quay (convey the healing states all at
   once: shutters open, lamps out, the fish slab full, the berth clear, people *out*). No
   puzzle — a victory walk past everything you mended.
4. **The ship docks.** Convey it threading the mouth (beacon + cleared berth paying off) and
   taking the berth; the dock master's long-empty register line finally gets a name.
5. **The vet ashore.** The outsider steps off — the first arrival in memory. Convey the town
   folding around them.

## AFTERNOON — help arrives
6. **The friend, in time.** Bring the vet home (or lead them). Convey the near-thing
   resolving — help reaching the friend at the edge of too-late. Land the recovery
   **ambiguously enough to feel earned**, not sentimental (see the ending-tone note).
7. **The partner, too.** Convey the vet going up to the foot cottage; the keeper's sick
   partner reached as well — the two mirrors resolved by one arrival. Easing the keeper's
   burden and lighting the beacon were the same act, all along. *(Quiet callback: the model
   boat by the partner's bed — the sea they loved — and now the boat that came.)*

## DUSK — the exhale
8. **The mother, rewarded.** Among those coming off the ship is **the one she waited for** —
   the empty place at her table fills, her window lamp joined by a lit doorway. Convey it
   in passing, not fanfare: hope kept was hope repaid, in parallel with the cure arriving.
   (Her lamp, all along, was the argument you finally came around to.)
9. **The town, alive.** The closing image mirrors the opening: the same camera that held a
   shut, grey, empty town now holds an open, lit, peopled one — the beam turning over it.
10. **The unexplained.** Convey that the cat never learns *why* the days froze — only that
   they've thawed. Love and fear, never fully told. The courage the whole arc taught: you
   broke the loop not when it was safe, but when staying had become the danger.

## Notes / open (ending tone — decide)
- **Recovery:** recommend the friend clearly *turns a corner* but leave a soft ambiguity so
  it reads as earned, not guaranteed.
- **Does the cat understand?** recommend **no** — it stays love and fear, unexplained.
- **Coda:** optional quiet epilogue beat (the friend well enough for the tidepools; the
  keeper tending a lit tower again) vs. cut to black on the living town — your call.
