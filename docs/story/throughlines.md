# Throughlines — Theme, Cohesion & Craft (whole-arc review)

The "as a whole" pass. What the game is *about*, how every system says it, how the setups
pay off, and the rules for what we show vs. tell and how we keep the player un-lost. This is
the lens the per-day docs (`day1.md`–`day9.md`), `arc.md`, and the design docs should all
answer to. Roles only.

## 1. Theme

**Thesis:** *Love that can't bear loss will freeze the world to keep what it loves — but the
only way to save what you love is to risk losing it. Hope is a choice, an act of courage,
and it's contagious.*

Corollaries the game keeps proving:
- **The loop is the fear made literal.** You froze time so tomorrow (and its risk) never
  comes. It feels like protection; it's slow abandonment.
- **Treating the symptom isn't curing the cause.** The loop delays; it never heals. (The
  rats — caught vs. sealed — say the same thing in miniature.)
- **Mending is love; the town heals one small kindness at a time.** But you can mend the
  whole town and still not mend the one you love — only *letting go* can.
- **Hope spreads.** The keeper can't hope until you refuse to stop; the town brightens fix by
  fix. Courage is caught, not argued.

## 2. Central metaphors (and the systems that carry them)

- **The dark beacon = withheld hope.** Relighting it = daring to call into the dark. It's in
  almost every frame; lighting it changes the whole silhouette.
- **The two lights** (a bedside lamp the keeper can still bear, the beacon they can't) =
  private care you can manage vs. public hope you can't. Plant Day 1 (one lit window under
  the dark lighthouse), name Day 2, pay off Day 8 (both burning).
- **The frozen day = the frozen heart.** Every loop is the same dawn because you won't let it
  move.
- **The imperfect loop / the leak = the truth the fear can't hold back.** The friend slips
  anyway, so "safe" was never safe.
- **The ship / the horn = the world returning.** Its absence is the game's defining silence;
  its arrival the exhale.

## 3. Character motivation (outer want · inner need · what they avoid · the turn)

| Role | Outer want | Inner need / wound | Avoids | The turn |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **You (orange cat)** | find help; relight the beacon | to not be powerless; to keep the friend | letting tomorrow come (risk) | choosing to let the day end — to hope out loud (Day 8) |
| **The friend** | rest; not to be a burden | to still be a person to you, not a patient; to spare you fear | leaning on you; being pitied | on the last night, letting the goodbye be true |
| **The keeper** | be left with their sick partner; be left alone | to never be crushed by hope again | the tower, the sea, your stubbornness (a mirror) | climbing the stairs beside you (Day 8) |
| **The mother** *(separate)* | the one she waited for, home from the sea | to keep hoping while the town gives up | admitting the sea won't give them back | they step off the Day-9 ship — hope rewarded |
| **The townsfolk** | keep their trade / routine | grief for the paused town they won't name | admitting it's over | each mended thing rekindles a little belief |

Inner-vs-outer, stated plainly: **the outer game is "relight the beacon so the ship can
dock." The inner game is "become able to let go."** They resolve in the same act.

## 4. The mirror structure (keep it tight)

**Two lone caretakers, one shape:**
- **You → the friend.** Alone with a sick companion; the one you can't fix by staying.
- **The keeper → their sick partner.** Alone with a sick companion too — the one *they* can't
  fix by staying, who gave up hoping.

Two cottages, two sick companions, two "small lights" (the friend by your window; the
keeper's bedside lamp). You are the keeper's past-self refusing to quit; the keeper is your
possible future if fear wins. Easing the keeper's burden and lighting the beacon are literally
the **same act** — the ship's vet saves both the friend and the partner. Never state this;
let the parallel caretaking and the two lights carry it.

**Three responses to one fear.** Around the caretaker mirror sits a triangle of ways to face
the fear of loss: the **keeper abandoned hope** (let the great light die), **you froze in
fear** (the loop), and the **mother kept hope alive** (a lamp always lit in her window for
someone the sea took). You travel from the keeper's pole toward the mother's — and her lamp
is the quiet proof, paid off on Day 9, that hope was right. She's not a third caretaker of
the sick; she's the town's stubborn hope.

## 6. The leak curve (canonical — keep consistent across days)

The friend's decline, shown only through the fish and small behavior — never a meter:

| Day | The fish / the tell |
|---|---|
| 1 | eats it; teases you; the ear stays up when faking sleep |
| 2 | eats slower, not all of it; quieter |
| 3 | eats less; the gift gets a fainter response |
| 4 | leaves most of it (even the good catch) |
| 5 | a bite, no more |
| 6 | picks at it; asleep when you get home |
| 7 | barely touched; the ear stays down; the goodbye-look |
| 8 | can't take it |
| 9 | barely stirs at dawn — the near-too-late |

The cruelty is timing: the town's fish supply is *fixed* (D5) right as they stop eating; you
learn the perfect gift right as they stop looking.

## 7. Gameplay ↔ theme cohesion (why each mechanic belongs)

- **Barrels (commit a direction, can't re-stand) + checkpoints:** committing is scary and
  irreversible — but you can retry *within a day*, and the day always ends. A mini-loop
  inside the macro-loop: safe practice, but time still moves. Choosing the tip = the courage
  to commit the loop keeps avoiding.
- **Catch the rats vs. seal the source:** treating symptoms vs. curing the cause — the whole
  premise in one quest. Sealing *stays fixed*; catching resets. That's the loop, indicted.
- **Persistence / the town heals:** the ache at the center — you can mend the entire town and
  it *stays* mended, but the friend keeps slipping. Fixing the world isn't the same as saving
  the one you love.
- **Carry one thing (in the mouth):** you can't hold everything; to pick something up you
  must set something down. Sequencing as small, constant sacrifice.
- **Phase gates / the tide:** the world has rhythms larger than you and they *move*. Working
  with time all game quietly argues that time must be allowed to move.
- **Outdoors = grid puzzle / indoors = free & quiet:** effort outside, feeling inside. The
  door is the exhale.

## 8. Show-implicit, tell-explicit (the clarity policy)

**Rule of thumb: feelings implicit, tasks explicit.**

- **Show, never state (narrative/emotion):** the loop's cause (unexplained, always); the
  friend's decline (the fish, the ear); the mirror (two lights, parallel caretaking); the
  town's grief (empty chairs, shuttered windows, the withheld horn); hope returning (lamps
  lit, chairs filled). If a beat is about a *feeling*, it is carried by an object, an action,
  or the environment — not narration explaining it.
- **State plainly (gameplay):** every day has one **clear objective** the player is directed
  to (an on-screen objective line / journal). Phase gates are **signposted** by a character
  line *and* a visible cue (water pulled back, shutters, the lamp). The current target can
  take a **light highlight** (reuse the `Outline`/attention-arrow system) and, if the player
  stalls, an escalating **hint** (a tip banner, then a stronger nudge). The **"Reset to last
  checkpoint"** is always available.
- **The seam:** a task can be crystal clear while its *meaning* stays unspoken — "catch the
  rat by the partner's bed" is an obvious instruction; that it mirrors your own caretaking is
  never said.

Each per-day doc now carries an **"At a glance"** block: *Objective (told plainly) · Shown,
not told · If stuck (hints).*

## 9. Mechanic teaching & difficulty curve (recap, with purpose)

move/talk/carry (D1) → push + plate/gate + jump-up (D2) → crate-bridge + stack-jump (D3) →
lure/trap + low-tide (D4) → carry+plate seal (D5) → **barrel** run + winch + high-tide (D6)
→ full contraption + interior use (D7) → capstone strike (D8) → walk/payoff (D9). One new
verb per day, every prior verb reused — the toolbox compounds toward the beacon exactly as
the story compounds toward the choice.
