# Day 2 ‚Äî Searching / Meet the Keeper (beat outline)

Day 2: the loop is now *known*. Searching for a cure, the first fix that stays, and the
**keeper** ‚Äî your mirror. **Beats are direction + intent, not scripted lines.** `[GAME]` =
mechanic foregrounded. Roles only. Cross-refs `throughlines.md`, `arc.md`, `cast.md` (the
keeper), `../design/gameplay.md`.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** clear the marina worker's jammed slip; then take the (now
  low-tide-open) cliff path up to the lighthouse.
- **Shown, not told:** the loop confirmed (the morning is identical ‚Äî you could move through
  it in your sleep); the keeper as your mirror (a lone caretaker; two lights; can't climb the
  tower); the sick partner glimpsed (a sailor's model boat by the bed ‚Äî planted for Day 9).
- **If stuck (hints):** the marina worker states the slip task and, once it's clear, points
  you up the path (tide's down now); a light highlight on the boom/plate and the ledge to
  jump.

## Purpose in the whole
Turn the loop from a reveal into a fact the cat *uses*; deliver the first **persistent** fix
(the hybrid save model starts here); and introduce the keeper so the mirror can run the rest
of the game. Teach push + plate/gate + jump-up.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **marina worker** (the slip); the **fish seller / grocer / caf√©
keeper** (revisited for a cure ‚Äî dead ends); the **keeper** and their **sick partner**
(met/glimpsed at the foot cottage).

## MORNING  *(the same silver dawn ‚Äî but you know it now)*
1. **Wake ‚Äî into the loop.** Identical framing to Day 1. Convey that the cat knows this
   morning cold ‚Äî every slat of light ‚Äî and that knowing doesn't dull it. They move with new
   purpose.
2. **Check on the friend.** Convey the near-word-for-word repeat (you could say it with
   them); they eat the fish but **slower, not all of it** (the leak, first notch). The
   ear-up tell repeats.
3. **The decision.** If the day won't move, you will ‚Äî today you search for help properly.

*‚Üí phase tick: MORNING ‚Üí MIDDAY.*

## MIDDAY  *(work the town; then the slip)*
4. **The search ‚Äî dead ends.** Quick revisits: the fish seller, the grocer, the caf√© keeper
   each, kindly, have no cure. Convey the same wall in every direction ‚Äî motivating the turn
   outward to the ship/light later.
5. **The jammed slip ‚Äî help the marina worker.** `[GAME] push + plate + boom.` The slip is
   blocked by empties and a jammed boom-gate. Weigh a **plate** with a crate to hold the boom
   open, push the remaining crates through, clear the slip. ‚Üí `slip_cleared`, `crates_freed`
   (first persistent fix). Convey the marina worker noticing the cat's stubbornness ‚Äî and
   pointing them at the old keeper up the point, now the tide's down.

*‚Üí phase tick: MIDDAY ‚Üí AFTERNOON.*

## AFTERNOON  *(the gold hour; the climb to the dark lighthouse)*
6. **The cliff path ‚Äî the step up.** `[GAME] jump.` A single ledge blocks the way; jump it ‚Äî
   the verb taught on flat stakes before the real climbs later. The one lit window at the
   tower's foot grows as you near.
7. **The foot cottage ‚Äî the keeper & the sick partner (glimpsed).** The keeper answers the
   door but won't step far from the bedside inside. Through the lit window: a bed, a still
   shape, a paper-thin cough ‚Äî and a **model boat / ship painting by the bed** (the partner
   was a sailor once; planted for Day 9). Convey the keeper's voice going soft and steady
   toward the bed, the way yours does with the friend.
8. **The keeper ‚Äî the mirror.** Convey, from the doorway: no help here; help comes off the
   water and nothing comes. And why the beacon stays dark ‚Äî not a switch: the lens fell, the
   oil's run out, the stair's washed away, and even lit it must be tended all night; they
   **can't leave their partner** for the hours (and nights) it would take, and no one else
   will do it. Convey the deeper truth too ‚Äî they've stopped believing it's worth it; you
   call into the dark and nothing answers, so you learn to stop calling. Convey the two
   lights (the bedside lamp they still tend, the great light they've let die) and that the
   cat understands it completely ‚Äî which is the worst part.
9. **Turned away.** The keeper sends the cat home to sit with their own while they can ‚Äî
   convey it as the only thing they still believe works. Convey the cat clocking what the
   keeper *can't* do alone (the climb, the vigil) ‚Äî a seed of "then I will."

*‚Üí phase tick: AFTERNOON ‚Üí EVENING.*

## EVENING
10. **The walk home (opt.).** Convey: two houses in this town keeping a small light for
    someone they can't fix. You pass the dark lighthouse thinking about lights and the ships that
    used to read them.

## NIGHT
11. **Home ‚Äî sleep.** The friend is quiet, glad you're back; they eat the fish **slower than
    yesterday, and not all of it**. Curl in ‚Üí fade ‚Üí loop.
12. **Wake ‚Äî loop to Day 3.** Same dawn; the keeper's words return before your eyes open
    (*nothing answers*) ‚Äî but the slip you cleared is still cleared. Some things you did are
    holding. You sit up faster today.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** `slip_cleared`, `crates_freed` (borrowable crates from now on).
- **Knowledge:** the tower path opens at low tide; the keeper won't light the beacon; someone
  is sick at the cottage (the mirror). Sets up Day 3.
- **Leak:** friend eats slower, not all of it ‚Äî the first visible notch.
