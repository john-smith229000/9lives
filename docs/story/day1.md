# Day 1 — The Normal Day (beat outline)

Day 1: the gentle establishing day — meet the town, feel its warmth and its helplessness,
sleep, and wake to the *same* dawn. **Beats are direction + intent, not scripted lines.**
`[GAME]` = mechanic foregrounded. Roles only. Cross-refs `throughlines.md` (theme &
foreshadow ledger), `arc.md`, `../design/locations_and_interiors.md`.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** a gentle day — go out, meet the town (the folk in each
  district), and come home to sleep. One optional favor (shove the marina worker's crate).
- **Shown, not told:** the town's warmth *and* its powerlessness; the dark tower with its
  one lit window (the two-lights seed); the friend's fragility; and — at dawn — the loop
  (the fish you left is gone, the café's second cup is full again).
- **If stuck (hints):** a soft tip to look around town; once you've met folk, a nudge to
  head home and rest; the way home is the lit path at dusk.

## Purpose in the whole
Establish stakes (the sick friend), the world (a harbor with the sea shut), and the loop —
and quietly **plant** the setups the later days pay off (see `throughlines.md` §5). Teach
move, talk, carry. No real puzzle; the weight is emotional.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **marina worker** (wharf); the **dock master** (office); the
**fish seller** (stall); the **grocer** (store); the **café/bookshop keeper**; the
**retiree(s)** (square); the **keeper** (glimpsed on the cliff path only).

## MORNING  *(silver light, fog low; no ship's horn — the absence is the sound)*
1. **Home — wake.** Open inside the cottage on the friend curled by the window. Convey
   closeness and worry through what you notice (their breath catching, then steadying) — not
   narration about fear.
2. **Check on the friend.** They deflect warmly and send you out; they eat the fish; the
   **ear stays up** when they fake sleep so you'll go (a tell to pay off later). They ask you
   to bring back "something to look at" (the gift ritual, planted).
3. **Out the door.** `[GAME] move.` Step into town; convey the quiet, the fog, the dark
   tower already at the edge of frame.

*→ phase tick: MORNING → MIDDAY.*

## MIDDAY  *(flat grey; the town, warm and stalled)*
4. **The wharf — the marina worker.** `[GAME] talk (+opt. push).` Convey kindness and the
   town's limit: no doctor for a cat here; help "comes off the island," and nothing comes.
   Boats sit ready in cradles with nowhere to sail (planted). Optional: shove a loose crate
   to its spot — the push tutorial, framed as a small kindness.
5. **The big dock — the register (seed).** The dock master is heads-down at their desk in the
   office by the dock; **jump up onto the desk** (onto the ledger) to get their grumbling
   attention. The open register shows a listed line they won't fill — a ship exists but can't
   come. Delivered as dry procedure.
6. **The fish market.** Only scraps today (the rats, planted). The seller presses a bit of
   fish on you for the friend — `[GAME] carry` it home now or later (the daily ritual).

*→ phase tick: MIDDAY → AFTERNOON.*

## AFTERNOON  *(one gold hour, then it drains)*
7. **The general store — the grocer (seed).** Half-empty shelves; a delivery lost since the
   storm, "half of it up a tree by the square" — pointed at, not yet actionable.
8. **The café / bookshop — the loop seed.** Convey the town's held breath ("the evenings
   just close over"); the two cups poured, one going cold (planted). Mood, no clue yet.
9. **The square & the cliff path.** The retirees remember when the light was lit (lore). The
   path up is blocked; above, the tower stands **dark but for one lit window** at its foot
   (the two-lights seed) — a shape moves at the rail and is gone. You can't get up. Not today.

*→ phase tick: AFTERNOON → EVENING.*

## EVENING  *(gold drains; fog climbs the streets)*
10. **A quiet beat (opt.).** The friend's favourite spot / the walk home. Pass **one window
    with a lamp lit and a table set for someone who isn't there** (the mother's — unnamed
    yet; the town's kept hope, planted). Kind hands, kind words, a bit of fish — and no cure
    in any of it. You've been half-turned toward home all day; now you go.

## NIGHT
11. **Home — sleep.** Give the fish and the day's gift; the friend curls up. Curl in beside
    them → fade to night → black.
12. **Wake — the same dawn.** Identical framing to beat 1. The loop lands through
    **specifics that reset**: the fish you left is gone from the dish; the café's second cup
    is full again; the register line still empty. You have been here already.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** memory only (Day 1 sets no physical flags — the hybrid model starts Day 2).
- **Plants (see `throughlines.md` §5):** the horn's absence, the dark tower + one lit window,
  boats in cradles, the two cups, the register line, the mother's window lamp + empty table
  setting, "help off the island," the gift ritual, the ear-up tell.
- **Leak:** baseline — the friend eats normally today; the decline begins Day 2.
