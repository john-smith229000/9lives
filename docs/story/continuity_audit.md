# Continuity Audit — logic holes & resolutions

A comb-through of the arc for "wait, that doesn't make sense" problems, and how each is
resolved. Where a fix changes canon, the affected docs are edited to match (noted). Roles
only.

## 1. The keeper must be the *sole* caretaker  *(the one that started this)*

**Hole:** the keeper's whole reason for not tending the light is that they can't leave their
sick loved one. But if a **mother** is also caring for that loved one (the earlier
"lighthouse family" version), then the keeper *isn't* trapped — so why not just go light it?
The character collapses.

**Fix:** the keeper cares, **alone**, for a **sick partner** (life companion) — no one else
can or will. This is a clean mirror of the player (alone with a sick companion). Two
cottages, two lone caretakers, two small lights. The **mother is decoupled** from the
lighthouse (see §2). *Applied: `cast.md`, `throughlines.md`, `day2/4/5/7`.*

## 2. "Why doesn't the keeper just light it?" — even alone

**Hole:** even as a lone caretaker, couldn't they nip up and light it once?

**Fix:** the beacon is **not a switch**. To light it at all someone must: recover the
**fallen lens** (in the square's tree), get **oil** (deliveries stopped), repair the
**washed-out stair**, haul it up, and keep an **all-night vigil** to hold the flame. That's
days of legwork plus nightly hours away from a bedside — impossible for someone who can't
leave a sick partner's side for long, and pointless in the eyes of someone who's **stopped
believing** it matters. The player can do it precisely because they're **free**: their own
companion sleeps through the day, and no one depends on the player minute-to-minute. *That's
why only the player can break the deadlock* — and it's the emotional point, not a
contrivance. *Applied across the chain quests + `day7`.*

## 3. The mother, recast

**Hole/loose end:** with the child/family removed, what is the "mother"?

**Fix (proposal):** a **separate townsperson** whose partner (or grown child) **sailed out
on the lifeline ship before the harbor shut** and is now stranded away — she embodies "the
sea keeps our loved ones from us" without another sickbed. Warm, worried, ordinary life
continuing. Her small quest is a **domestic nuisance** (the pantry rat of Day 4 moves to
*her* house). Decoupled from the keeper. *Applied: `cast.md`, `day4`.* Flag if you'd rather
cut her entirely.

## 4. The three-caretakers structure was over-egged

**Fix:** simplify to the **two lone caretakers** (you ↔ the keeper). The mother is a lighter
"waiting on the sea" echo, not a third sickbed. *Applied: `throughlines.md` §4.*

## 5. The ship — what it is and why it can't come

**Hole:** why is a vet on a ship to a cut-off town, and why can't it just arrive?

**Fix:** the ship is the town's **regular lifeline run** — supplies, mail, and a **circuit
vet/medic** on its rounds — not something summoned. It can't **dock** because the **berth is
fouled** and can't **find the harbour mouth** because the **beacon is dark**: its approach
falls on the **night high tide** (the deep berth only floods then, this season), so it must
thread a fogged, rocky mouth **in the dark** — and without the light it turns back out to
sea, every time. This also explains the town's slow withering (its lifeline stopped).
*Applied: `STORY.md`, `objects_and_kinetics` tide note already supports it.*

## 6. Why not arrive in daylight?

**Fix:** tied to §5 — the deep-water berth is only reachable on the **high tide, which falls
at night** this season. Daylight = too little water over the berth. Hence the light is
essential. (Also lets the tide system carry real weight.)

## 7. How has the town survived being cut off?

**Fix:** it's been cut off for **weeks / a season**, not years — the town is **withering,
not dead** (the "paused" state). Keep the retirees' "when the light was lit" memories
**recent** (this season), not decades gone. *Note for writing: don't imply years.*

## 8. Fish, with the harbor shut

**Fix:** **local shore-fishing** continues — hand-lines and pots off the quay yield a little,
poor and dwindling — while **nothing trades in or out** by ship. The rats make the scarce
catch worse. So "only scraps today" holds up. *Note in `day1/day4`.*

## 9. Persisted fixes vs. NPCs who don't remember

**Hole:** if the town resets each dawn but repairs persist, the marina worker wakes to a slip
he doesn't remember clearing — isn't that strange?

**Fix — lean into it:** yes, and that strangeness is a **feature**. The town resets except
the things you've mended, so the world quietly, inexplicably **improves** around people who
don't recall doing it — and **that** is exactly the wrongness the **café/bookshop keeper**
half-senses (the loop-awareness thread *is* the persistence system felt from inside). Ties
two systems together instead of leaving a hole. *Applied: `throughlines.md`, `arc.md`
loop-awareness thread.*

## 10. What actually breaks the loop?

**Hole:** if `beacon_lit` persists, why doesn't the ship just come the next loop after Day 7?

**Fix:** lighting the beacon (+ cleared berth) is **necessary but not sufficient**. The loop
is bound to the **cat's refusal to let tomorrow come**; even with everything ready, fear
keeps re-sleeping into the same dawn. The loop breaks only when the cat **chooses** to let
the day end for real — **Day 8's turn** (light it *and* let go, the same night). **Day 9** is
the released tomorrow. *Applied: `day7` (ready, not lit) → `day8` (lit + the choice) →
`day9`.*

## 11. The leak (friend declines in a "frozen" loop)

**Not a hole — an intended mystery.** The freeze is centred on the friend and is **failing at
exactly the thing it's for**; that's the ache, left unexplained (love and fear, never told).
Keep it as the one thing the reset can't hold. *Documented, `throughlines.md` §6.*

## 12. Can the cat dodge the reset by not sleeping?

**Fix:** the day ends by **choosing** to curl up beside the friend out of love — an emotional
beat, not a trap. Staying out doesn't stop time in any way that helps; the loop is bound to
the cat's refusal, not to a sleep timer. (Fable logic; don't over-explain in-game.)

## 13. Orphaned plant: the child's boat-drawing

**Fix:** repurpose to the keeper's sick partner, who was a **sailor/fisher** — a **model
boat / a ship painting by the bed**. Pays off Day 9 (the ship that finally comes is the sea
they loved / once worked). *Applied: `day2` glimpse, `day9` callback, `throughlines` ledger.*

## 14. Tending the light "forever"

**Out of scope, noted:** one lit night (Day 8 → 9) is enough for the arrival; the game ends
when the ship comes and the crisis breaks. Post-loop, the healed town resumes its own
tending. No need to model a perpetual vigil.

## 15. Symmetry to reinforce (not a hole — a strengthening)

Two cottages, two sick companions, two "small lights": the friend by your window and the
keeper's bedside lamp. Your home has its small light too — the mirror is literal and quiet.
Lean on it in the art + camera (both cottages framed with one warm window against the grey).
