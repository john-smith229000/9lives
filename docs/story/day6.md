# Day 6 — The Berth (beat outline)

Day 6: turning the town's face back to the sea — clear the berth so the ship can tie up.
**Beats are direction + intent, not scripted lines.** `[GAME]` = mechanic foregrounded.
Cross-refs `arc.md`, `chain_quests.md` (Day 6), `../design/barrel_and_checkpoints.md`.
Roles only.

## At a glance
- **Objective (told plainly):** clear the fouled berth at the big dock so a ship can tie up
  (work in daylight; the wreck lifts on the evening tide).
- **Shown, not told:** the town beginning to believe; the dock master's brittle armor
  cracking.
- **If stuck (hints):** the dock master states the jam and the tide/daylight rules; highlight
  the ramp barrel and the winch; the rising tide is visible.

## Cast in play
The **friend** (home); the **dock master** (the berth + register); the **marina worker**
(crates/barrels); the town at the edges (beginning to notice).

## MORNING
1. **Home — wake.** Same dawn; fish to the friend (leak steady). The cat heads for the big
   dock with the day's aim clear: a light is no use if the ship can't berth.
2. **Big dock — the problem.** The dock master shows the fouled berth — a **sunk skiff /
   debris** jamming it. Convey his brittle procedure and the impossibility he's resigned to.
   Establish the timing rules by his mouth: the wreck only lifts on the **evening tide**, and
   the crew only help while there's **daylight**.

*→ phase tick: MORNING → MIDDAY.*

## MIDDAY  *(daylight — the work window)*
3. **Break the jam.** `[GAME] barrel run.` A heavy **barrel** stands at the ramp top. **Tip
   it down the ramp** in the correct axis so it rolls and **knocks the debris loose**. A
   wrong tip sends it into the sea → **reset to checkpoint** and retry. Convey the barrel's
   commitment (you can't re-stand it).
4. **Winch it up.** `[GAME] crank + plates.` **Crank the capstan/winch** to raise the
   loosened skiff partway; wedge **crates onto plates** beneath to **hold it**. Convey the
   contraption logic — hold it, then move it.

## AFTERNOON  *(the tide begins to make)*
5. **Wait for the tide / clear it.** `[GAME] high-tide gate (signposted).` The skiff only
   floats free on the rising **evening tide**; with it wedged and the tide lifting, push it
   clear of the berth. → `berth_cleared`.
6. **The dock master cracks.** Convey the first fracture in his armor: a berth he can finally
   log as clear — the pen that's hovered over an empty line for the whole game finally has
   somewhere to go (not filled yet; that's Day 9). The town edges toward believing.

*→ phase tick: AFTERNOON → EVENING.*

## EVENING
7. **The town notices (ambient beat).** Convey small healing: a lamp or two more lit, a chair
   filled at the café, a nod from someone who didn't nod before. Head home.

## NIGHT
8. **Home — sleep.** The friend, weaker, glad you're back; convey how the outside progress
   and the inside decline pull against each other. Curl up → fade → loop.
9. **Wake — loop to Day 7.** The berth stays clear. Only the light remains — and the cat
   knows the last of it runs through the keeper.

## Persists / advances
- **Persists:** `berth_cleared` (the ship can now dock, if it can find the mouth).
- **Town heals:** debris gone, gangway down, the derrick rigged; more lamps; the café fuller.
- **Leak:** steady decline; the tension between town-mending and friend-fading sharpens.
