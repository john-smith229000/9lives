# Quests, Obstacles & the Nine-Day Web

Revised design pass per notes. **No proper names** — everyone is referred to by role until
you name them (the friend cat included). No kitten. The narrative docs (`../story/cast.md`,
`../story/day1.md`, `../story/arc.md`) follow the same nameless cast.

The goal here is your ask: **creative obstacles that interconnect and change across days.**

## The spine (what the whole web is for)

Relight the **beacon** so the incoming ship (carrying the traveling vet) can find the
harbour in the fog — **and** clear the **berth** at the big dock so it can actually tie up.
Beacon lit + berth clear = the ship docks (Day 9) = the friend (and the keeper's partner)
gets help. Everything below is a link in getting there.

## The goals, and what every task actually pays toward them

Two hard goals win the game; one soft goal is the heart. Nothing is busywork — each task
yields one of five currencies that ladder toward these:

- **G1 — relight the beacon.** Needs: the **lens** (a part), **oil** (fuel), a **usable
  stair** (access), the **tower key** (access), and the **flame** (the choice).
- **G2 — clear the berth.** One physical job (the sunk skiff), gated by tools + tide.
- **G3 — comfort the friend (and the partner).** *Never a cure* — only the ship's vet cures.
  This is your daily agency and the emotional throughline: fish, the small gift, your
  presence. It keeps them going; it doesn't fix them. (That's the whole ache.)

**The five currencies a task can pay in:**

| Currency | What it does | Earned by (examples) |
|---|---|---|
| **Information** | reveals *what/why/where* (never physical progress) | dock register (a ship is *due*), café (it carries a **vet**; the beacon's needs; the rat-hole's location), retirees (lore) |
| **Access** | lets you *reach* the next task | freed crates (slip) → bridges/stacks; permanent bridge; the **found key** → the tower; cleared rat-source → reliable fish |
| **Parts** | a literal beacon component | the **lens** (tree) |
| **Fuel/tools** | consumables the goals need | **oil** (beacon), the **rat trap** |
| **Goodwill** | opens people (and, through them, places) | **comforting the keeper's partner** → deeper into the cottage (where the key is) → the keeper eventually joins you |

So the accounting is clean: **info tells you what to do; access/parts/fuel physically build
G1 and G2; goodwill opens the cottage and the keeper; and the good fish you win along the way
feeds G3.** The friend never improves from your tasks — the tasks are all aimed at getting
the *ship* in, which is the only thing that can.

## The dependency web (obstacles that unlock each other)

This is the heart. Each obstacle is small, but they **gate one another**, so the town
unlocks like a knot coming loose. Arrows = "needed for."

                                        ▼

                          (cliff path to the beacon; a high ledge; a tree)
                                        │

             │                          │                             │
             ▼                          ▼                             ▼
   return goods to grocer      (needs lens + oil + key       seal the source →
   → grocer gives LAMP OIL      to actually relight)          RATS GONE for good
     + a RAT TRAP                                             (fish seller, mother,
             │                                                 retiree, keeper's loved
             ▼                                                 one all improve at once)
   catch rats (trap) for the
   fish seller / mother / retiree ──► good FISH from the seller
             │                          │
             ▼                          ▼
   bring FISH + catch the rat     bring FISH to the friend (daily comfort)
   scaring the keeper's loved one       + the fish seller shares the SHIP SCHEDULE
             │                                    (the vet — learned ~Day 3)
             ▼

Relight = **oil** (grocer, via the tree) + **lens fitting** (fell into the town — see the
tree beat) + **clear stair/bridge** (crates → made permanent by the marina hand) + **tower
key** (keeper's trust). Ship docks = beacon lit **and** berth cleared.

## Developing your seed ideas

- **Boxes to bridge / jump up (near the lighthouse).** The marina hand's crates. Early you
  improvise a *rickety* box-bridge across the cliff gap (resets each loop). Once you've
  helped the marina hand, they build a **proper plank** there that **persists** — the puzzle
  literally becomes a permanent shortcut. The same crate-stacking reaches a high ledge and a
  tree elsewhere, so one verb (push/stack + jump) services three obstacles.
- **Get something from a tree.** A storm knocked the **brass lens fitting** off the beacon
  and it lodged high in the big tree in the square (poetic: a piece of the light literally
  fell into town). Reaching it needs stacked crates + a jump. Returning it up the tower is
  a late-chain beat. *(Variant: the grocer's oil crate is what's stuck in the tree — pick
  one, or use both: lens in the tree, oil on a ledge.)*
- **Bring fish to the sick friend.** The daily ritual + soft tutorial for fetch/deliver.
  The friend perks up cosmetically. But **good** fish depends on the rat problem: early days
  the seller only has picked-over scraps; after you help with the rats, real fish. So a
  comfort beat quietly teaches that fixing the town helps the friend too.
- **Help the fish seller.** Their catch is being stolen by **rats**. Help → good fish (for
  the friend) + they share the **ship schedule / the vet** (they've been counting the days).
- **Catch a rat (for the mother / retiree / keeper's loved one).** The standout evolving
  mechanic — see below.

## Recurring mechanics that CHANGE across days (the loop payoff)

The loop only sings if things transform run to run. Three examples:

1. **The rats — chore → permanent fix.**
   - Days 4–5: you *catch* individual rats (a timing/lure mini-action with the trap) for the
     fish seller, the mother, and the retiree. Satisfying, but **they come back each loop** —
     because you're treating symptoms.
   - The turn: the café keeper or a retiree lets slip there's a **hole behind the wharf**.
     Reaching it needs the box-bridge. **Sealing it is a persistent fix** — and it improves
     several yards at once (fish seller's catch safe, the mother's pantry and the retiree's
     shed quiet, the keeper's sick partner calmed). One clever fix, a visible ripple.
     This is "small kindnesses hold the place together" made mechanical.

2. **The box-bridge — improvised → permanent.**
   - First built rickety and it resets; after the marina-hand favor it becomes a real plank
     that stays. Later loops you just *walk across* where you once puzzled — the town
     literally getting easier as it heals.

3. **The fish — scraps → good catch → shared with the keeper.**
   - Scraps early (rats) → good fish once the rats are handled → then you're not just
     feeding the friend but the keeper's loved one too, which is what earns the key. The
     same small act widens outward.

## Nine-day shape (a rhythm, not a rigid script)

Each mid-day introduces/uses one mechanic and ends on a **persistent** change (hybrid
persistence). Knowledge always carries; listed fixes carry via flags; daily rituals (fish
to the friend) and NPC/prop positions reset.

- **Day 1 — the normal day.** Establish; meet the town by role; first fish-to-friend ritual;
  notice the rat, the dark lighthouse, the stranded things. *Persists: memory only.* Ends: sleep
  → wake to the same dawn (the loop lands).
- **Day 2 — searching.** Help the marina hand (jammed slip) → **crates freed** (persists).
  Glimpse/meet the keeper and their sick loved one. Bring fish again.
- **Day 3 — reaching.** Box-bridge + jump → the tree (**lens fitting**) and the grocer's
  stranded stock → **oil + trap** (persist). The fish seller / café keeper reveal the ship
  carries a **traveling vet**.
- **Day 4 — the goal locks.** The objective crystallizes: beacon dark + berth fouled =
  no ship. Begin the rat-catching (fish seller, mother, retiree).
- **Day 5 — the clever fix.** Find + **seal the rat source** (persists) → four characters
  improve; reliable good fish. Start easing the keeper's burden (fish + the rat by their
  loved one's bed).
- **Day 6 — the berth.** Help the dock master clear the fouled berth (crates + leverage) →
  **berth usable** (persists).
- **Day 7 — the beacon.** Marina hand makes the **bridge permanent**; reinstall the **lens**
  (from the tree) and haul **oil** up; keeper's trust → **tower key**. Everything but the
  flame.
- **Day 8 — the turn.** One last piece; you and the keeper both choose to hope — light it at
  dusk, knowing it means letting tomorrow finally come.
- **Day 9 — the ship.** Beacon holds, berth clear, fog parts, the ship docks; the vet; the
  friend (and the keeper's loved one) is helped. The loop breaks.

## Loop & persistence rules (so the web works)

- **Always persists:** knowledge (implicit — the premise).
- **Persists via flags (physical):** `slip_cleared`, `crates_freed`, `bridge_permanent`,
  `oil_secured`, `lens_recovered`, `rat_source_sealed`, `berth_cleared`, `keeper_trust`,
  `has_tower_key`, `beacon_ready`, `beacon_lit`.
- **Resets each loop:** the fish you bring the friend (ritual), individually-caught rats
  (until the source is sealed), NPC positions, single-day puzzle state.
- **The town heals visibly.** Tie ambient state to the flags: cleared berth, mended bridge,
  quiet (rat-free) market, more townsfolk out, warmer light — so progress is *seen*, not
  just tracked. This is the emotional reward of persistence.

## Revised Day 1 (nameless, real cast, no kitten)

Same gentle town-tour shape as before, re-cast:

- **Morning:** wake beside the friend; check on them (they deflect, send you out); the
  **marina hand** at the wharf — isolation, "help comes from off the island," a first crate
  to shove back (optional push).
- **Midday:** the **dock master** (the register lists a vessel *due* — the ship-seed as
  bureaucracy); the **fish seller** (grey seas; only scraps today — the rats; presses a bit
  of fish on you for the friend).
- **Afternoon:** the **grocer** (dwindling stock; a delivery lost since the storm — points at
  the tree without you acting yet); the **café/bookshop keeper** ("the evenings just close
  over" — the loop seed); the **cliff path** — the dark lighthouse, the keeper a distant shape at
  the rail, the way up blocked.
- **Evening:** a quiet beat (the friend's favourite spot); the ache of a warm town that
  can't help. Home.
- **Night:** curl beside the friend → fade to night. Wake to the **same dawn** — landed
  through specifics that reset (the fish gone, the seller's scraps back, the bowl full
  again). *You have been here already.*

Nothing on Day 1 persists but memory. The obstacles above are only *noticed* today — the
stuck delivery, the rat, the dark lighthouse, the blocked path — so Days 2+ have somewhere to go.
