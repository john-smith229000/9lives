# Interactive & Kinetic Objects + Phase Gates

Gameplay-first object design. Confirms the **carry-one-thing** rule, reframes the
placeholder crate/ball as specific diegetic items, invents a harbor-town kit of kinetic
objects (each with a *reason to be there*), and lays out **signposted** phase-timed
mechanics. Cross-refs `gameplay.md`. Nameless roles.

## Guiding lens: a harbor with the sea shut

Since nothing comes in or out, **the town's life has stalled in place** — empties pile on
the wharf with nowhere to ship, boats sit grounded, oil is hoarded for a light no one tends.
So the kinetic objects aren't abstract "crates and balls"; they're **the town's stuck life**,
and moving them is the town starting to move again. Every object below gets a one-line
reason it's sitting where it is.

## Carry — one thing, in the mouth

The cat holds **exactly one** small object at a time, in its mouth. While carrying it
**can't push, jump-with-it, or carry a second thing** (jumping drops it, or is disallowed).
This is a puzzle constraint: you must **sequence** trips (you can't bring the key *and* the
fish; fetch the trap *then* the bait). Pick up / drop / **give** (I on a target).

| Carried item | From → to | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Fish** | market → friend / keeper's loved one / trap-bait | the daily ritual + bait |
| **Rat** (live, caught) | a yard → out past the wharf / a bin | you carry the caught rat away in your mouth |
| **Brass lens fitting** | the tree → tower | small, precious; the light, piece by piece |
| **Oil can** (small) | a tapped barrel → the lamp tank | the barrel is rolled; the can is carried up |
| **Tower key** | keeper → tower door | can't also carry fish — sequence it |
| **Flint & tinder** | grocer/hearth → the lamp | the capstone light |
| **A small gift** | a beach/shelf → friend | "bring me something to look at" |

## Kinetic objects (pushed / rolled / floated)

Reframing the placeholders and adding a harbor kit. **Verb** = how it moves; **why** = its
diegetic reason for sitting there.

| Object | Verb | Behaviour | Why it's there |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fish crates** *(was: crate)* | push | slide 1 tile; **float**; **stack** | the day's empties, piled because nothing ships out — the closed harbor made physical |
| **Barrels** (oil / salt / ale) | **push OR roll** | upright = slide; **knock over = rolls** like a ball, gains speed on slope; floats | oil hoarded for the dark beacon; salt for curing the fish that can't be sold |
| **Mooring buoy** *(was: ball)* | roll | rolls on land, **floats** on water; light | pulled in off the moorings since no ship ties up; now underfoot on the wharf |
| **Lobster / crab pots** | push | light; **stack**; float; you can bait/trap with one | stacked unused; ties thematically to the rat trap |
| **Handcart / barrow** | push | a mobile platform: **one heavy item rides on it** | how the grocer/marina moved goods, now idle |
| **Driftwood plank** | carry/drag | bridges a 1-tile gap; seals the rat-hole | washed up; the town's stopped clearing the strand |
| **Grounded skiff / sunk debris** | winch + push | **heavy**; **floats at high tide**; platform or blockage | fouling the big berth; the wreck of the stalled harbor |
| **Ice block** | push | slides; **melts over the day** (phase timer) | the fishmonger's ice, wasted now the catch can't ship |
| **Sacks** (grain / salt) | push | heavy (slow); can weigh a plate | grocer's stock, going stale |

Engine note: **barrel = ball + crate** (rolls when toppled, pushes/floats when upright) —
one new object covers most of the kit. Buoy = a ball that floats. Handcart = a crate that
holds a carried item. Ice = a crate with a melt timer keyed to the phase.

## Mechanisms (use / lever)

| Mechanism | Verb | Does | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capstan / winch** | crank (use) | raises the sunk skiff; hoists a load | the big dock |
| **Derrick + cargo net** | load + crank | lifts a crate/lens up to the tower path or dock | dock / cliff foot |
| **Sluice / sea-gate** | pull lever | floods or drains a basin (with the tide) | between marina + rocks |
| **Beacon lamp** | valve + crank + shutter + flame | oil → lens → wind → strike | tower top |
| **Pressure plates + gate/boom** | weigh (crate/barrel/self) | holds a gate open while weighted | marina slip, tower vent |

## Phase-timed mechanics — always signposted

You wanted several, all obvious. Each is telegraphed by a **character line** and/or an
unmissable **contextual cue** (water level, shutters, the lamp). Time advances by progress,
so these are "come back at the right phase," not a stopwatch.

| Gate | Open when | Signpost |
|---|---|---|
| **Low-tide causeway** to the rocks / rat-hole / tidepools | midday–afternoon | fish seller: "Tide's out past the point come midday — that's your only window to the rocks." + you can *see* the water pulled back |
| **High-tide float** — the grounded skiff lifts | evening | dock master: "She'll lift off the mud on the evening tide, not before." + the skiff visibly rises |
| **Market open** / good fish | midday | cook & fish seller mention hours; stall shutters up at midday, down otherwise |
| **The derrick / dock crew** work | daylight only | dock master: "Crew's gone by dusk — do it while there's light." |
| **Ice before it melts** | carry it *before* afternoon | fishmonger: "It'll be water by the afternoon, so be quick." |
| **The beacon** | night (that's the point) | the whole game |

Design rule: **never gate progress on a phase without a line or a visible cue in the same
scene.** The player should always think "ah, come back at low tide," never "how was I
supposed to know."

## Reframed placeholder puzzles (concrete)

- **The jammed slip (Day 2).** *Fish crates* (empties) block the marina hand's slip; a
  *barrel* has rolled against a **boom-gate**. Weigh a **plate** with the barrel to hold the
  boom, push the crates through, clear the slip. → crates freed.
- **Reach the tower (Day 3).** Push freed *fish crates* into the cliff gap (they **float**)
  to bridge it; **stack** two against the square's tree and **jump** to knock the *lens
  fitting* loose; **carry** it off.
- **The ball, reframed.** Wherever a "roll it to the target" beat wants to exist, it's a
  *barrel* (knock over → rolls down the wharf slope onto a plate / into the water to bob as
  a stepping-float) or a *mooring buoy* — not an abstract ball.

## Which objects each day leans on

- **D1:** fish (carry), one fish crate (push tutorial).
- **D2:** fish crates + a barrel + plate/boom (slip); jump.
- **D3:** floating fish-crate bridge; crate-stack + jump (tree → lens); handcart optional.
- **D4:** fish (bait) + trap + rat (carry the caught rat away); **low-tide** window to reach
  a nest on the rocks.
- **D5:** driftwood plank + a crate on a plate (seal the hole); low-tide access.
- **D6:** capstan/winch + crates on plates + the grounded skiff on the **evening tide**.
- **D7:** roll oil *barrels* to the tower foot → **carry** oil cans up; derrick to hoist the
  *lens*; valve + crank; **daylight** for the dock crew's help.
- **D8:** carry flint → strike the flame (night).
- **D9:** walk; the horn; the ship on the tide.

## Engine mapping (build economy)

- **Extend the ball** → the **barrel/buoy** (roll, slope, float; barrel also pushes upright).
- **Extend the crate** → **fish crate** (float+stack already exist), **handcart** (holds a
  carried item), **ice** (melt timer), **sacks** (heavy = slower push).
- **Extend the goal pad** → **held-while-weighted plate** driving a **gate/boom**.
- **New:** carry/give, rat + trap, capstan/derrick/sluice (`use` mechanisms), tide state
  (drive water height + walkability off the day phase), persistent-repair mesh swaps.
