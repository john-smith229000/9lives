# The Chain Quests — Days 4–7 (puzzle designs)

The middle of the game: each day, one townsperson's problem is a concrete puzzle that
removes one obstacle between the town and a lit beacon, and ends on a **persistent** unlock.
Gameplay-first; the story beat rides on top. Uses the toolkit in `objects_and_kinetics.md`,
`barrel_and_checkpoints.md`, and `gameplay.md`. Roles only. Cross-ref `quest_web.md` (the
dependency graph) and `../story/arc.md` (the emotional weave).

Format per quest: **problem → objects → solution steps → phase/timing → persists → story
beat**. `[GAME]` marks the mechanic foregrounded.

---

## Day 4 — The rats  *(fish seller + the foot cottage + a retiree/mother)*  `[GAME] lure & trap`

**Problem.** Rats are eating the fish seller's catch (so you've only had scraps to bring the
friend), troubling the keeper's **sick partner** at the foot cottage, and nesting in a
retiree's shed / the mother's pantry. A few small catches teach one verb.

**Objects.** the **rat trap** (from the grocer, Day 3); **fish** (bait, carried one at a
time); the caught **rat** (carried out in your mouth); a **low-tide causeway** to a nest on
the rocks.

**Solution (each catch is a small positioning puzzle):**
1. Carry a **fish** to the trap and prime it *(bait)* — you can hold the fish **or** the
   trap-approach, not both, so you sequence the trips (the carry constraint doing work).
2. Stand so the rat's only flee-path from its corner runs **into** the baited trap (herd it
   with your approach angle; walls and crates shape the lane).
3. Collect the caught rat and **carry it out** — to the sea wall / beyond the last house
   (a small kindness beat: you relocate it, you don't kill it — colours the cat).

**Phase/timing.** The rocks nest is only reachable at **low tide (midday)** — the fish
seller signposts it: *"Tide's out past the point by noon; that's your window, love."*

**Persists.** Knowledge of where they nest. The individual catches **reset each loop** —
they keep coming back, because you're treating the symptom. (Sets up Day 5.)

**Story beat.** The catches converge on the mirror: the fish seller (grateful, gruff); the
**foot cottage**, where you catch the rat by the keeper's **sick partner's** bed while the
keeper, nearby, *notices*; and the retiree / mother (a shortcut + a scrap of lore; the mother
keeping a lamp lit for someone the sea keeps away).
> **Mother:** You got it — oh, bless you, it's been keeping the little one awake for nights.
> **Keeper:** *(watching, quiet)* …That was kind. You didn't have to.

## Day 5 — Seal the source  *(the clever fix)*  `[GAME] carry + plate-weighted seal`

**Problem.** The rats never stop because there's a **hole behind the wharf** they pour out
of. Close it and four yards go quiet at once.

**Objects.** a **driftwood plank** (carried); a **fish crate** + a **pressure plate** (to
weigh the plank shut) — or tip a **barrel** onto it; the **box-bridge** / low-tide access to
reach the hole.

**Solution:**
1. Cross to the hole (box-bridge from the freed crates, at **low tide**).
2. **Carry** the plank to the hole and set it over the opening.
3. It won't hold alone — push a **crate onto the plate** beside it (or tip a **barrel** so
   it comes to rest against the plank) to **weigh it shut**. *(plate-holds → sealed)*

**Persists.** `rat_source_sealed` — and the ripple is the point: the fish seller's catch is
safe (**reliable good fish** from now on), the retiree's shed and the mother's pantry quiet,
and the keeper's **foot cottage** calms. **One fix, several lives eased** — "small kindnesses
hold the place together," made mechanical.

**Story beat.** With real fish at last, you start bringing it up to the keeper's **sick
partner** too, and the rat by the bed is already gone. The **keeper's thaw begins** — and the
cruel timing lands: you finally have the *good* fish, and your friend has started to leave it.
> **Keeper:** Quiet in there tonight. First in a long while. …I don't know how you did the
> half of it. *(a pause)* Come by tomorrow, if you're coming.  *(→ keeper_trust begins)*

## Day 6 — The berth  *(the dock master)*  `[GAME] barrel run + winch + plates`

**Problem.** Even with a light, the ship can't tie up — the big berth is fouled by a **sunk
skiff / debris**. Clearing it turns the town's face back to the sea.

**Objects.** an **oil barrel** at the ramp top; the **capstan/winch** (crank); **crates** on
**plates** (to wedge); the debris; the **evening high tide**; **daylight** dock-crew help.

**Solution (a 3-step contraption):**
1. **Tip the barrel down the ramp** so it rolls (correct axis!) and **knocks the jam loose**
   — the barrel's signature move; a wrong tip sends it into the sea → *reset to checkpoint.*
2. **Crank the winch** to raise the loosened skiff partway.
3. Push **crates onto the plates** beneath to **hold it up**, then shove it clear of the
   berth. Timing: the skiff only lifts on the **evening high tide** (dock master signposts:
   *"She'll lift off the mud on the evening tide, not before"*), and the crew only help
   while there's **daylight**.

**Persists.** `berth_cleared`.

**Story beat.** The dock master, all procedure, finally has a line he can fill — the first
crack in the armour.
> **Dock master:** …Berth's clear. First time I've written that in this ledger in — *(stops,
> pen hovering)* —well. Long enough. *(nearly a smile)*

## Day 7 — The beacon  *(the keeper)*  `[GAME] everything combined`

**Problem.** Assemble the light: oil, lens, a clear stair, and the will to try. Gated behind
the keeper's trust.

**Gate — find the key.** You don't get handed a key — you **find** it. Comforting the
keeper's **sick partner** (carry **fish**, set the bedside **trap**, and — the real
thing — **curl up beside them**, the way you do your friend) is what earns you deeper into
the cottage; and there, by the bed / on its old hook, is the **tower key the keeper stopped
wearing**. Picking it up opens the locked **tower door**. The mirror made literal: doing for
their loved one what you do for yours is exactly what puts the key in your paw.

**Objects.** the **oil barrel** (rolled to the tower foot, **tapped** → carried **oil cans**
up); the **lens fitting** (from the tree, Day 3 — carried up, **seated**); the **permanent
bridge/stairs** (the marina worker builds it in **daylight** this day → `bridge_permanent`);
the lamp's **crank** (raise the lens) and a **plate-held vent/shutter**.

**Solution (multi-step, reusing every verb):**
1. The marina worker plants a **permanent plank** over the old box-bridge gap (persist) — the
   climb is now a walk.
2. **Roll the oil barrel** to the tower foot; **tap** it; **carry** oil cans up and **fill**
   the tank (several trips — the one-item carry paces it).
3. **Carry the lens** up; **seat** it in the mount.
4. **Crank** the hoist to raise the lens into the lamp; hold the **vent** open with a crate
   on a **plate**. Everything is ready — but the flame.

**Persists.** `bridge_permanent`, `beacon_ready`, `has_tower_key`.

**Story beat.** The keeper lets you into the lantern room and voices the despair you've been
carrying — the **low point** seeds here.
> **Keeper:** …You've really done it. Oil, glass, the stair. All but the fire. *(quiet)*
> Light it if you like. I'll tell you what I know: nothing comes when you call. You'll stand
> here in the dark and learn how far you can fall. *(a breath)* …And still you're reaching
> for the flint. God help you. God help us both.

## How the chain locks together (quick view)

```
Day 3 grocer's oil + trap, tree's lens  ─┐
Day 4 rats caught (symptom)              ─┤
Day 5 rat SOURCE sealed → good fish,     ─┤→ keeper's trust
      cottage calm                        │
Day 6 berth cleared (ship can dock)      ─┤
Day 7 key + oil + lens + bridge          ─┴→ beacon READY (all but the flame)
Day 8 the flame (the choice)  →  Day 9 the ship
```

Each day borrows the tool the previous day unlocked (freed crates → bridge → reach the tree
& the hole; the trap → the rats → the good fish → the keeper's trust → the key). Nothing is
busywork; every fix is a link.
